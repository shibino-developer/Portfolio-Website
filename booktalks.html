Jay Shetty

Think like a Monk

Jay Shetty’s Think Like a Monk is a compelling guide to personal transformation, offering insightful wisdom and practical strategies rooted in ancient philosophy. The book's accessible approach and relatable stories make it a valuable resource for anyone seeking to lead a more balanced, mindful, and fulfilling life.
Favourite Quote: “The more you have, the more you want. Happiness is not a state of having but a state of being.”
USP: Think Like a Monk combines timeless monastic wisdom with contemporary insights to guide readers toward inner peace, resilience, and personal fulfillment, making it a practical and inspirational guide for modern living.

A P J Abdul Kalam

Wings of Fire

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam’s Wings of Fire is an inspiring and motivational read that details the remarkable life of one of India's most respected figures. The book combines personal anecdotes with a compelling narrative of scientific achievement and national pride, making it an uplifting and enlightening account of overcoming adversity and achieving one's dreams.
Favourite Quote: “Dream, dream, dream. Dreams transform into thoughts, and thoughts result in action.”
USP: Wings of Fire offers a powerful and inspirational autobiography of Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, showcasing his extraordinary journey from humble beginnings to becoming a key figure in India's scientific and technological progress, and motivating readers to pursue their dreams with passion and determination.

R.K. Narayan

Malgudi Days

Malgudi Days is a celebrated collection of short stories by R.K. Narayan, first published in 1943. Set in the fictional town of Malgudi, the book offers a vivid portrayal of everyday life in India. Through a series of engaging and relatable stories, Narayan captures the essence of Indian society, highlighting the quirks, challenges, and aspirations of its characters. The stories explore a range of themes, including social norms, human relationships, and the simplicity of rural life. The book masterfully portrays the charm and complexity of small-town India with warmth and humor. Each story provides a snapshot of life in Malgudi, showcasing Narayan’s keen observation and gentle storytelling. The collection's rich characterizations and relatable situations make it a cherished work that resonates with readers of all backgrounds.
Favourite Quote: “The world is full of two kinds of people: those who can make you laugh and those who make you cry. I think the best people are those who can do both.”
USP: Malgudi Days offers a rich tapestry of life in a fictional Indian town, using charming, insightful stories to reflect the universal and timeless aspects of human experience.

Jonathan Swift

Gulliver's Travels

Jonathan Swift's Gulliver's Travels (Vol. 1, 2) is a masterful satire that critiques contemporary society, politics, and human nature. Through imaginative and often absurd scenarios, Swift provides sharp commentary on the follies and vices of his time. The novel's allegorical depth invites readers to reflect on the human condition, making it both an entertaining adventure and a thought-provoking read.
Favourite Quote: "When I consider the little space I occupy in this world, the narrow limits of my life, and the great extent of my travels, I am much less disturbed by the many faults of mankind."
USP: Gulliver's Travels uniquely blends fantastical adventures with sharp satire to critique human nature and societal flaws, making it a timeless classic in political satire.
